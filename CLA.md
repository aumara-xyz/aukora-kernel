## Recommended mechanism: DCO (lightest), with a CLA only if counsel wants assignment/relicensing rights

Two common options — recommend starting with the **DCO** unless counsel wants future relicensing/commercial-dual-license
flexibility (then a CLA):

### Option A — Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO) [recommended default]
Contributors add `Signed-off-by: Name <email>` to each commit, certifying the standard DCO 1.1 text (they wrote it or
have the right to submit it under the project's license). No copyright assignment; lowest friction; widely understood.
Enforced by a sign-off check in CI.

### Option B — Contributor License Agreement (CLA)
A signed agreement granting the project entity (e.g. Aumara) a license to the contribution and the right to relicense.
Heavier; appropriate if a **dual-license / commercial track** is planned (the operating plan mentions one). Requires:
- the **receiving entity** named (Aumara? Peter Viviani? a future company) — Peter + counsel decide,
- a patent grant clause (important given the pending filing — Guard/counsel should align this with the patent strategy),
- a signing flow (CLA-assistant bot or manual).
